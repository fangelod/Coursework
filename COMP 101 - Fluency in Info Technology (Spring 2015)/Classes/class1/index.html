Primates

Overview

Primates include humans, apes, monkeys, and prosimians. Many of them may be familiar, but they also include less familiar animals such as lemurs, which are an ancient, relatively primitive group.

Definitions

Primate
A mammal of the order Primates. They are distinguished by having hands, handlike feet, and forward-facing eyes.
Ape
Any of various large, tailless Old World primates of the family Pongidae. They include the chimpanzee, gorilla, gibbon, and orangutan.
Monkey
A primate of the Haplorrhini suborder and simian infraorder, excluding apes. There are both Old World and New World monkeys.

World's Most Endangered Primates List

This list of highly endangered primate species is selected and published by scientific specialists. The purpose of the list is to attract the attention of the public and to stimulate national governments to do more. Species are selected for the list based on two primary reasons:

  • extremely small population sizes
  • very rapid drops in numbers

The most endangered South American primates are

  1. cotton-top tamarin
  2. variegated spider monkey
  3. yellow-tailed woolly monkey
