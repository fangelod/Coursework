A big part of what Kasama does is host montly events. The events that happen each month has varied from year to year but there are a select few that are hosted every year. In order to attend these events, people must pay membership dues that last the entire year. If they choose not to pay dues, they must pay for each individual event. Membership dues are $5 for the entire year, but $2 for each event. Considering there are at least 8 events for the years, you'd being saving a lot of money just to pay dues instead! Below you can find the events that we host every year:

General Body Meeting

The general body meeting is the first event that Kasama hosts every year. This is where we explain what Kasama is all about and serve delicious Filipino food! People gather around, play ice-breaker games, chat, and have an overall good time. This event usually happens in the first or second week of classes. You can find out more about the event here.

Halo-Haloween

Halo-Haloween is an event we host that happens every October In the past we talk about the importance of Haloween in the Philippines while serving a sweet Filipino desert. It is call "halo halo," hences the play on words for the event. Halo halo has all sorts of different ingredients in it, such as shaved ice, condensed milk, and coconut. You can find out more about the event here.

Kasama White Elephant

Our white elephant is just that, white elephant. For those that don't know, white elephant is a gift exchanging game where each person that participates buys a gift. Everyone has a number, which determines the order that they go in. Each person either randomly chooses a gift or can steal a gift that has already been chosen. Once a gift has been stolen three times, it cannot be stolen again. Once everyone has gone, the game is over. Kasama hosts white elephant right before winter break. You can find out more about the event here.

Fear Factor

Fear factor is an event that Kasama hosts every year that introduces how weird Filipino cuisine can get. We serve popular dishes that seem disgusting to the normal bystander like "dinuguan" or "balut." And of course we have to make a competition out of it. You can find out more about the event here.

Kasama Beach Trip

Beach Trip! This event is usually the last event of the year. We go to the same place every year in April. It's a fun weekend where everyone bonds, sings karaoke, and sunbathes on the beach. You can find out more about the event here.
