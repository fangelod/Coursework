Basketball at the Collegiate Level

What is the "NCAA"?

The NCAA stands for the National Collegiate Athletic Association. It is the collective the regulates the schools that participates in sports at the collegiate level in the United States. One of the sports the the NCAA oversees is basketball. It is split up into 3 divisions, of which many are used watching division 1 basketball on television. Every March, NCAA hosts a national tournament and the best teams participate in it. It generates a lot of buzz with predictions of who's gonna win, even the president participates in filling out a bracket.

Rules and Regulations

Basketball's rules change between the different associations. In the NCAA, a few rules are different from basketball played at the professional level.

  • Total Time: two 20 minute halves
  • Shot Clock: 35 seconds
  • 3 Point Line: 19'9"
  • Width of Paint: 12 ft
  • Fouls: 5 personal fouls until fouling out
  • Possessions: Possession arrow
