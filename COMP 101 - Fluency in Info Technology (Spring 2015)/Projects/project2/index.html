The Sport of Basketball

What is "Basketball"?

Basketball is a team sport where teams of five try to shoot a ball through what is called a "hoop." The sport is played on a rectangular court that consists of two hoops. A team can score by getting the ball through their opponent's hoop. The amount of points received from each score varies depending on the distance from the hoop that the shot was taken from. The only exception to this rule is what is known as free throws. Free throws are taken as a result of a player being fouled while attempting a shot or if the opposing team reaches a certain number of team fouls. Each free throw made is worth one point.

Basketball's Influence

Sports has taken a large influence in the world today, generating entertainment, camaraderie, and revenue. Professional basketball in America alone generates 5 billion dollars per year. Because of this, some people even aim to earn a living by playing the sport. Aside from basketball played for monetary reasons, basketball has become one of America's favorite pastimes. It has been incorporated in physical education classes in schools and people of all ages come together to play the sport.
