Landmarks

Natural Landmarks

The Philippines is home to many natural landmarks, of which are one of the attractions of tourists that come to the country. The background image found on this website is an image of the Banaue Rice Terraces. These terraces were carved into the mountains of Ifugao 2,000 years ago. It is known as the "Eighth Wonder of the World" to Filipinos. The Philippines is a major rice exporter and is 8th largest rice producer in the world. To this day, Filipinos still grow rice in these terraces.

Urban Landmarks

The Philippines doesn't have many urban architectural landmarks but it does have something to make up for it. The one thing that the Philippines does big are shopping malls. In 2006, the SM Mall of Asia opened in Bay City, Pasay, Philippines in which its floor area spanned 4,380,500 square feet. It is the 10th largest mall in the world. In 2008, the SM City North EDSA in Quezon City, Philippines was redeveloped and is the fourth largest mall in the world. Its total floor area spans 5,197,660 square feet. In 2011, the expansion of SM Megamall made it third largest mall in the world. The SM Megamall's floor area spans 5,451,220 square feet.
