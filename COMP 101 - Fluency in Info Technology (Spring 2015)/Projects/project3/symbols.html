Filipino Flag

The Filipino flag consists of three main parts. Its main distinguishing feature is the white equilateral triangle containing an eight-ray sun with three stars in yellow. The white triangle symbolizes equality and fraternity. The sun symbolizes unity, freedom, people's democracy, and sovereignty. Each of the eight rays represents one of the first eight provinces. To the right of the white triangle are its blue top and its red bottom. The blue stripe symbolizes peace, truth, and justice. The red stripe symbolizes patriotism and valor. An interesting fact about the filipino flag is that when the flag is flipped upside down so that the red is on top and the blue is on bottom, it indicates that the country is at a state of war. The flag was adopted on June 12, 1898.

National Anthem

Lupang Hinirang, or Chosen Land in English, is the national anthem. It was composed by Julian Felipe in 1898 and its lyrics were adapted from a Spanish poem. Originally, the anthem was instrumental, of which was adopted on June 12, 1898. The anthem with lyrics was officially adopted in 1963. Just like the Pledge of Allegiance in the United States, all students recite it before classes start. The audio clip provided in the table to the left is the instrumental version.
