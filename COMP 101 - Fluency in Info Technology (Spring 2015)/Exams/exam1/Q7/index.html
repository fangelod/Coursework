Computer Science Buildings

Computer Science has had a number of homes in UNC

  • West House
  • New West
  • Sitterson
  • Brooks Building
  • West House: The one-story brick building was built in 1935 by Rutherfordton textile magnate Kenneth Tanner to house his sons when they entered the University.
  • New West: Built in 1859, New West was so named because it was much newer than Old West, which dated from 1823.
  • Sitterson Hall: Sitterson Hall, which opened in 1987 and is named for former University Chancellor J. Carlyle Sitterson,
  • Brooks Building: The Brooks Building was dedicated in 2008 and named for the Department’s founding chairman, Frederick P. Brooks Jr.
